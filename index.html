Document Checklists

Required Documents by Visa Type

Every document category is critical. Missing even one item can lead to rejection. Below are the standard requirements — our team verifies your specific file before submission.

🇪🇺

Schengen Visa Documents

Required for all 27 Schengen zone countries (Germany, France, Italy, Spain, Netherlands, Austria, etc.)

⬇ Download Checklist PDF
🪪

Personal Documents

  • ✓
    Valid Passport — Original + all old passports. Must have min. 2 blank pages & be valid 3+ months beyond travel dates.
  • ✓
    Passport-Size Photos — 2 recent photos (35×45mm, white background, taken within last 6 months).
  • ✓
    National Identity Card (CNIC) — Photocopy of front and back.
  • ✓
    Visa Application Form — Completed, signed, and dated Schengen visa application form.
✈

Travel Documents

  • ✓
    Flight Itinerary / Round-Trip Booking — Confirmed or dummy round-trip flight reservation showing entry and exit dates.
  • ✓
    Hotel / Accommodation Proof — Hotel bookings, Airbnb confirmations, or host's invitation letter with their ID.
  • ✓
    Travel Insurance — Minimum coverage €30,000 for medical emergencies, valid for all Schengen countries and entire trip duration.
  • ✓
    Detailed Day-by-Day Itinerary — A clear travel plan for each day of your stay.
💰

Financial Documents

  • ✓
    Bank Statement (Last 6 Months) — Original, bank-stamped. Must show sufficient balance (min. €50–70 per day of stay recommended).
  • ✓
    Bank Certificate / Letter — On bank letterhead confirming account holder's name, account number, and available balance.
  • ✓
    Salary Slips / Income Proof — Last 3–6 months' salary slips from employer (if salaried).
  • ✓
    Tax Returns / FBR Documents — If self-employed or business owner, income tax returns for the last 1–2 years.
  • ✓
    Sponsor's Financial Documents — If someone else is sponsoring the trip, their bank statements, employment letter, and affidavit of support.
💼

Employment / Business Documents

  • ✓
    Employment Letter (NOC) — On company letterhead: position, salary, leave approval dates, and confirmation of return to job.
  • ✓
    Business Ownership Proof — NTN, business registration certificate, chamber membership (if self-employed).
  • ✓
    Leave Approval Letter — From employer confirming approved leave dates matching your travel period.
🏠

Ties to Home Country

  • ✓
    Property Documents — Property ownership papers showing you have assets in Pakistan and strong reason to return.
  • ✓
    Family Ties Evidence — Marriage certificate, children's birth certificates, or family registration certificate.
  • ✓
    Previous Travel History — Copies of all previous visas and entry/exit stamps from any country.
⚠️

Important: Requirements may vary slightly by embassy (German, French, Italian, etc.). Our team verifies your complete file against the specific consulate's current requirements before submission.

🇨🇦

Canada Visitor Visa (TRV) Documents

Temporary Resident Visa for tourism, family visits, or business travel to Canada.

⬇ Download Checklist PDF
🪪

Personal & Identity Documents

  • ✓
    Valid Passport — Must be valid for the entire duration of the planned stay in Canada.
  • ✓
    CNIC / Family Registration Certificate (FRC) — Original CNIC plus NADRA FRC for family applications.
  • ✓
    Passport-Size Photographs — Recent passport photos as per IRCC specifications.
  • ✓
    Birth Certificate — Required if applying with family members or for child applicants.
  • ✓
    Marriage Certificate — If including spouse in application (Nadra-issued or court-attested).
💰

Financial Documents

  • ✓
    Bank Statement (Last 6 Months) — Original, bank-stamped. Consistent balance demonstrating ability to fund the trip.
  • ✓
    Bank Certificate — Confirming relationship with bank, account type, and current balance.
  • ✓
    Salary Slips (Last 3–6 Months) — For salaried individuals as proof of regular income.
  • ✓
    Income Tax Returns (FBR) — Last 2 years' filed tax returns demonstrating financial standing.
  • ✓
    Property / Investment Portfolio — Proof of assets: property papers, shares, mutual funds, vehicle ownership, etc.
💼

Employment / Business Proof

  • ✓
    No Objection Certificate (NOC) — From employer on letterhead with job title, salary, leave approval, and guaranteed return to position.
  • ✓
    Business Registration Certificate — If self-employed: NTN, SECP registration, or chamber of commerce certificate.
  • ✓
    Business Bank Statement — Company's bank statement for business owners showing active operations.
🇨🇦

Canada Host / Invitation Documents

  • ✓
    Invitation Letter from Host in Canada — If visiting family/friends: their letter, Canadian PR/Citizenship, and proof of address.
  • ✓
    Host's Status in Canada — Copy of Canadian PR card, citizenship certificate, or passport.
  • ✓
    Accommodation Proof — Hotel bookings or host's address proof if staying with family/friends.
✈

Travel & Purpose Documents

  • ✓
    Flight Itinerary — Booked or dummy round-trip reservation clearly showing arrival/departure dates.
  • ✓
    Travel Itinerary / Purpose Letter — Detailed letter explaining purpose of visit, planned activities, and commitment to return.
  • ✓
    Previous Travel History — Copies of all prior visas (especially USA, UK, Schengen) significantly strengthen Canada TRV applications.
💡

Pro Tip: Canada places strong emphasis on ties to home country and financial stability. A consistent 6-month bank history with no sudden large deposits is critical. Our team helps you build the strongest possible financial narrative.

🇦🇺

Australia Visitor Visa (Subclass 600) Documents

For tourism, family visits, or business travel to Australia. Applied online via ImmiAccount.

⬇ Download Checklist PDF
🪪

Identity & Personal Documents

  • ✓
    Valid Passport (Scanned Copy) — Bio-data page. Passport must be valid for the duration of your stay.
  • ✓
    CNIC (Scanned) — Front and back of national identity card.
  • ✓
    Digital Passport Photo — Recent photo meeting Australian immigration specifications.
  • ✓
    Marriage / Birth Certificates — If applying with family members (must be translated to English if in Urdu).
💰

Financial Evidence

  • ✓
    Bank Statements (Last 6 Months) — Showing sufficient funds to cover the entire trip. Australia expects clear, consistent financial history.
  • ✓
    Bank Letter / Certificate — Confirming account details and current balance.
  • ✓
    Salary Slips / Proof of Income — Last 3 months' payslips or business income evidence.
  • ✓
    Tax Returns (FBR) — Demonstrates established financial presence in Pakistan.
  • ✓
    Assets Proof — Property papers, vehicle ownership, investments showing you have reasons to return.
💼

Employment / Occupation

  • ✓
    Employment Letter — On company letterhead: designation, salary, leave approval, and confirmation you will return to your position.
  • ✓
    Business Evidence — If self-employed: NTN, business registration, bank statement showing business activity.
✈

Travel Purpose & Plans

  • ✓
    Travel Itinerary / Purpose Statement — Clear explanation of your visit purpose, planned activities, and accommodation details.
  • ✓
    Flight Bookings (Return) — Dummy or confirmed round-trip flight reservations.
  • ✓
    Hotel / Accommodation Confirmation — Bookings or invitation letter from Australian host with their status documents.
  • ✓
    Previous International Travel — Copies of all prior visas especially USA, UK, Schengen. Significantly strengthens Australian applications.
🏥

Additional Requirements

  • ✓
    Health Insurance — Proof of adequate health insurance for the duration of your stay in Australia.
  • ✓
    Character Declaration — You may be asked to provide a police clearance certificate (PCC) from Pakistan.
⚠️

Note: Australia's visitor visa is entirely online via ImmiAccount. There is no embassy appointment. Processing typically takes 15–60 days. Our team manages the complete online application on your behalf.

🇬🇧

UK Standard Visitor Visa Documents

For tourism, family visits, medical treatment, or short business trips to the United Kingdom.

⬇ Download Checklist PDF
🪪

Personal Documents

  • ✓
    Valid Passport — All old passports must also be submitted. Passport must cover entire intended stay.
  • ✓
    CNIC — Photocopy of both sides of national identity card.
  • ✓
    Passport-Size Photos — As per UKVI specifications (45mm × 35mm, light grey or cream background).
  • ✓
    Family Documents — Marriage certificate and children's birth certificates (if applicable).
💰

Financial Documents

  • ✓
    Bank Statement (Last 6 Months) — Original stamped. UK Home Office scrutinizes transactions carefully — avoid large unexplained deposits.
  • ✓
    Bank Certificate — Official letter from bank on letterhead confirming account status and balance.
  • ✓
    Salary Slips (Last 6 Months) — For salaried employees to demonstrate consistent income.
  • ✓
    Income Tax Returns — FBR tax returns for the past 2 years (especially important for self-employed applicants).
  • ✓
    Property / Asset Evidence — Ownership documents showing financial roots in Pakistan.
💼

Employment / Business

  • ✓
    Employer's NOC / Leave Letter — Confirming role, salary, leave approval, and guaranteed return to employment.
  • ✓
    Business Registration / NTN — For business owners: full documentation of business ownership and activity.
  • ✓
    Company Financial Statement — Business bank statements and accounts showing viable business operations.
✈

Travel Plans & Purpose

  • ✓
    Cover Letter — Detailed personal letter explaining purpose, itinerary, accommodation plan, and clear intent to return.
  • ✓
    Flight Itinerary (Return) — Round-trip flight booking confirming travel dates.
  • ✓
    Accommodation Proof — Hotel reservations or UK host's address proof and invitation letter.
  • ✓
    UK Contact / Sponsor Letter — If visiting family or friends: their UK status documents, address proof, and support letter.
  • ✓
    Prior Visa History — Copies of Schengen, US, Canadian, or other prior visas. Excellent travel history greatly strengthens UK applications.
💡

UK Tip: The UK Home Office places exceptional weight on your cover letter and the "genuine visitor" test. Our team crafts precise, compelling cover letters tailored to your specific circumstances and travel history.

🎓

European Student Visa Documents

General requirements for student visas to Germany, France, Austria, Italy, Poland & other European countries.

⬇ Download Checklist PDF
🪪

Personal Identity Documents

  • ✓
    Valid Passport — Must be valid for at least 6 months beyond the visa expiry date. All old passports to be included.
  • ✓
    CNIC / NICOP — Pakistani national identity card or NICOP for overseas Pakistanis.
  • ✓
    Passport-Size Photos — Recent biometric photos meeting the specific embassy's specifications.
  • ✓
    Birth Certificate — Nadra-issued or equivalent, may need to be attested / apostilled.
🏫

Academic & Admission Documents

  • ✓
    University Admission Letter — Official letter of admission from the European university confirming your enrolled program.
  • ✓
    Academic Transcripts & Certificates — Attested copies of your degree(s), HEC-attested transcripts and certificates.
  • ✓
    APS Certificate (Germany Only) — Academic Evaluation Centre certificate verifying Pakistani academic qualifications for German universities.
  • ✓
    Language Certificate — IELTS, TestDaF, DELF, or equivalent language proficiency test results as required by your university.
  • ✓
    Statement of Purpose (SOP) — A strong, university-specific personal statement explaining your motivation and study plans.
💰

Financial Proof of Subsistence

  • ✓
    Blocked Account (Germany) — €11,208 minimum deposited in a German blocked account (Sperrkonto) via Fintiba, Coracle, or Deutsche Bank.
  • ✓
    Scholarship Letter — Official award letter if you have a DAAD, Eiffel, Erasmus+, or institutional scholarship.
  • ✓
    Sponsor's Financial Documents — If family-sponsored: bank statements, CNIC, salary slips, property papers, and formal sponsorship declaration (Verpflichtungserklärung for Germany).
  • ✓
    Bank Statement (Self-funded) — Applicant's or sponsor's recent 6-month bank statement showing adequate funds.
🏠

Accommodation & Insurance

  • ✓
    Proof of Accommodation in Europe — Confirmation of student dormitory, private rental agreement, or host's address confirmation.
  • ✓
    Health / Travel Insurance — Comprehensive health insurance valid in the destination country for at least the first 90 days.
✈

Travel Documents

  • ✓
    Flight Booking / Itinerary — One-way or return flight booking showing planned arrival date in the destination country.
  • ✓
    Visa Application Form — Completed and signed national visa (long-stay) application form for the specific country.
🇩🇪

Germany Note: Germany requires the APS certificate before applying for a student visa — this process takes 6–10 weeks. Start early. Our team coordinates the entire timeline including APS, blocked account, TestDaF registration, and visa appointment simultaneously.

How Pakistani Students Can Study in Germany Tuition-Free in 2025

Germany is home to over 400 public universities offering world-class education at zero or near-zero tuition fees. For Pakistani students, this is the single greatest opportunity in international education today.

Why Germany? The Case for Pakistani Students

German public universities charge between €0 and €350 per semester — not per year, per semester. Compare this to the UK (£15,000–£35,000/year) or Australia (AUD 20,000–45,000/year) and the difference is staggering. Many Pakistani families can realistically afford to send their children to Germany when they cannot afford other Western countries.

Germany also has one of the strongest job markets in Europe, particularly for engineers, IT professionals, and medical researchers — fields where Pakistani graduates excel.

Step 1: Get Your APS Certificate (Start Here First)

The APS (Academic Evaluation Centre) certificate is mandatory for Pakistani students applying to German universities. This certificate verifies your Pakistani academic qualifications and is required before your student visa can be processed.

  • Apply at the APS Pakistan office in Islamabad
  • Submit your degree certificates, transcripts, and CNIC
  • The process takes 6–10 weeks — start this before anything else
  • Cost: approximately PKR 25,000–30,000
  • You cannot get a German student visa without this certificate
⚠️ Travxo Tip: Start your APS application the moment you decide to study in Germany. Do not wait until you have a university admission — the 6–10 week wait can delay your entire timeline by one intake semester.

Step 2: Language Requirements

Most German public university programs are taught in German. You will need to prove language proficiency through one of these tests:

  • TestDaF — most widely accepted, minimum TDN 4 in all sections
  • DSH — offered by universities themselves, DSH-2 required
  • Goethe-Zertifikat C1/C2 — accepted by many universities
  • IELTS/TOEFL — only for English-taught Masters programs

Growing numbers of Masters programs (especially in engineering and IT) are now offered entirely in English — these only require IELTS 6.5 or TOEFL 90.

Step 3: Find and Apply to Universities

Use the DAAD University Database (daad.de) to search programs by subject, city, language, and tuition. Apply directly to each university — Germany has no central application portal for international students (unlike France's Campus France).

Key intake deadlines to know:

  • Winter Semester (October) — Apply between April and July. This is the main intake.
  • Summer Semester (April) — Apply November to January. Fewer programs available.

Step 4: Open a Blocked Account (Sperrkonto)

Germany requires international students to prove they can financially support themselves. You must deposit €11,208 (as of 2025) into a German blocked account. You can only withdraw a fixed monthly amount once in Germany.

Approved providers for Pakistani students:

  • Fintiba — fully online, fast, English-language support
  • Coracle — popular alternative, online process
  • Deutsche Bank — requires a branch visit in Germany

Step 5: Apply for Your Student Visa

Once you have your APS certificate, university admission letter, and blocked account confirmation, you can apply for a German National Visa (Type D) at the German Embassy in Islamabad or Consulate in Karachi.

Documents required:

  • Valid passport + copies of all old passports
  • APS certificate
  • University admission letter
  • Blocked account confirmation (€11,208)
  • Health insurance valid in Germany
  • Proof of accommodation in Germany
  • Language test certificate
  • Completed visa application form
  • 2 recent passport-size photos

DAAD Scholarships for Pakistani Students

The DAAD (German Academic Exchange Service) offers fully funded scholarships for Pakistani students at the Masters and PhD level. Benefits include:

  • Monthly stipend of €850–€1,200
  • Health insurance coverage
  • Travel allowance
  • One-time study allowance

Applications open in October each year for the following academic year. Contact Travxo early to prepare a strong DAAD application.

Schengen Visa Refusal: Top 10 Reasons for Pakistanis & How to Fix Them

A Schengen visa refusal is not the end. At Travxo, we have successfully reversed refusals for hundreds of Pakistani applicants. Here are the top 10 reasons embassies refuse Schengen visas to Pakistanis — and exactly how to fix each one.

Reason 1: Insufficient Financial Proof

The most common reason. Embassies want to see that you can comfortably fund your entire trip without needing to work. The minimum recommended balance is €50–70 per day of travel. A bank balance of €1,500 for a 10-day trip is typically not enough — you need consistent history, not just a current balance.

Fix: Submit 6 months of bank statements showing consistent balance. Avoid sudden large deposits — they raise red flags. Include a bank certificate on letterhead confirming your balance.

Reason 2: No Proof of Ties to Pakistan

Embassies must believe you will return home after your visit. If you have nothing tying you to Pakistan — no property, no stable job, no family dependents — they may fear you intend to overstay.

Fix: Include your employment NOC letter, property documents, vehicle ownership, family registration certificate, and evidence of assets in Pakistan.

Reason 3: Weak or Missing Cover Letter

Many Pakistanis skip the cover letter or submit a generic one. This is a critical mistake. The cover letter is your chance to tell the embassy exactly who you are, why you are visiting, and why you will return.

Fix: Write a detailed, specific, honest cover letter. Include your travel purpose, day-by-day itinerary, accommodation details, who is funding the trip, and your reason for returning to Pakistan.

Reason 4: Inconsistent or Missing Documents

Even one missing document can lead to refusal. Dates that don't match across documents (flight dates vs hotel dates vs leave letter dates) are an immediate red flag.

Fix: Cross-check every document for consistent dates and information. Use Travxo's document checklist and have an expert review your file before submission.

Reason 5: No Prior International Travel History

A completely blank passport is harder to approve — not impossible, but harder. Embassies use prior travel as evidence of trustworthiness.

Fix: If you have any prior visas (even expired ones), include them. If it's your first travel, make your other documents — especially financial and employment — exceptionally strong.

Reason 6: Purpose of Visit is Unclear

Vague travel purposes ("tourism" with no itinerary) are red flags. Embassies want specifics.

Fix: Prepare a detailed day-by-day itinerary with specific cities, activities, and accommodation. Show you have genuinely planned this trip.

Reason 7: Suspicious Bank Transactions

A large cash deposit made just before your visa application is one of the most common reasons for refusal. Embassies call this "window dressing" and view it as deceptive.

Fix: Never make large deposits just before applying. If someone is sponsoring you, document it properly with an affidavit of support and their financial documents — not a cash transfer into your account.

Reason 8: Travel Insurance Issues

Minimum coverage of €30,000 is required. Policies that don't explicitly state coverage for all Schengen countries are rejected.

Fix: Purchase travel insurance from a recognized provider explicitly stating €30,000+ medical coverage valid across all 27 Schengen member states for the exact duration of your trip.

Reason 9: Wrong Embassy Selected

You must apply to the embassy of your main destination country. Applying to the German embassy when you are spending most time in France is a procedural error that can cause refusal.

Fix: Apply to the embassy of the Schengen country where you will spend the most nights. If equal, apply to the first country of entry.

Reason 10: Previous Overstay or Immigration Violation

Any prior overstay in any country, or any immigration violation, will be a significant obstacle to any Schengen application.

Fix: Be completely honest in your application. Travxo can help you build the strongest possible case given your specific history and advise on realistic timelines.

Best European Scholarships for Pakistanis 2025: DAAD, Eiffel, Erasmus & More

Hundreds of Pakistani students receive fully funded European scholarships every year. Here is your complete guide to the top scholarships — what they cover, who qualifies, and exactly how to apply.

1. DAAD Scholarships (Germany)

The German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD) is the largest scholarship provider for international students studying in Germany. Pakistani students are among the top recipients.

  • Coverage: Monthly stipend €850–€1,200, health insurance, travel allowance, one-time study allowance
  • Level: Masters, PhD, postdoctoral research
  • Eligibility: Above-average academic record, relevant work experience for some programs
  • Deadline: October–November each year for the following academic year
  • Apply at: daad.de/en/study-and-research-in-germany/scholarships

2. Eiffel Excellence Scholarship (France)

Offered by the French government through Campus France, the Eiffel Scholarship is one of the most prestigious fully funded awards for international students.

  • Coverage: Monthly stipend €1,181 (Masters) / €1,400 (PhD), accommodation allowance, return flight, health insurance, cultural activities
  • Level: Masters and PhD
  • Fields: Engineering, economics, management, law, political science
  • Deadline: January each year — your French university nominates you
  • Key fact: You must first gain admission to a French university — they nominate you for Eiffel, not you applying directly

3. Erasmus+ Scholarships (EU-Wide)

Erasmus+ is the European Union's flagship education program. Pakistani students can benefit through two pathways:

  • Erasmus Mundus Joint Masters: Fully funded Masters programs across multiple EU countries. Stipend €1,400/month + travel allowance. Apply directly to the program.
  • Erasmus+ ICM (International Credit Mobility): Exchange semester at a European university if your Pakistani university has a partnership agreement.
  • Deadline: Varies by program — typically October to January
  • Apply at: eacea.ec.europa.eu/erasmus-plus

4. HEC Overseas Scholarships (Pakistan Government)

The Higher Education Commission of Pakistan funds scholarships for Pakistani students to study abroad, including in Europe. This is government funding — you study abroad but your fees and living costs are covered by HEC.

  • Coverage: Full tuition + monthly stipend + return airfare
  • Level: PhD and postdoctoral (primarily)
  • Requirement: 3 years of service in Pakistan after return
  • Apply at: hec.gov.pk/scholarships

5. Finnish Government Scholarships

Many Finnish universities offer 50–100% tuition waivers to high-performing international applicants. Unlike a separate application, these are awarded automatically based on your admission application strength.

  • Aalto University: up to 50% waiver for strong applicants
  • University of Oulu: ~50% waiver possible
  • University of Turku: up to 100% waiver for merit students
  • Application deadline: December–January for September intake

6. Italian DSU Grants (Income-Based Tuition Reduction)

Italy's DSU (Diritto allo Studio Universitario) system provides income-based tuition reductions and grants to international students. Pakistani students with modest family incomes often qualify for significant reductions — sometimes bringing tuition to €0.

  • Apply after university admission through the university's DSU office
  • Submit your Pakistani income documents (translated and notarized)
  • Reductions can include tuition waiver + meal vouchers + accommodation subsidy

Canada Visitor Visa for Pakistanis: Complete 2025 Application Guide

Canada is one of the most sought-after destinations for Pakistani families — visiting relatives, tourism, or family sponsorship. Here is a complete, honest guide to the Canada Visitor Visa (Temporary Resident Visa) for Pakistanis in 2025.

What Canada Looks For in Every Application

IRCC (Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada) assesses every Pakistani application against two core questions: Can you financially support your visit? And will you return home when your visa expires? Your entire application must answer both convincingly.

Complete Document Checklist

  • Valid passport — must cover your entire intended stay
  • CNIC + Family Registration Certificate (FRC) — NADRA issued
  • Bank statements — last 6 months — original, bank stamped, consistent balance with no sudden large deposits
  • Bank certificate — on letterhead confirming account number, type, and balance
  • Salary slips — last 3–6 months — for salaried applicants
  • FBR income tax returns — last 2 years — demonstrates financial standing
  • Employment NOC letter — confirming your role, salary, leave approval, and guaranteed return
  • Property documents / assets — property papers, vehicle, investments showing you have reasons to return
  • Invitation letter from Canadian host — if visiting family or friends
  • Host's Canadian status documents — PR card, citizenship certificate, or passport copy
  • Flight itinerary — round-trip booking
  • Accommodation confirmation — hotel or host's address proof
  • Previous travel history — copies of USA, Schengen, UK visas if any

The 6-Month Bank Statement Rule

Canada is very strict about financial documentation. Here is what visa officers look for:

  • Consistent balance over 6 months — not just a high balance today
  • No suspicious large deposits in the weeks before applying
  • Regular salary credits or business income visible
  • Sufficient funds to cover flights, accommodation, and daily expenses for the entire trip
Travxo Tip: Start preparing your bank statements 6 months before you plan to apply. Do not transfer large amounts from family members into your account just before applying — this is detected and causes refusal.

Prior Visa History — Your Greatest Asset

If you have previously been issued a US, UK, Schengen, or Australian visa — even expired — include copies of all of them. Prior visa approvals from trusted countries are one of the strongest signals a Canadian officer looks for. They significantly increase your approval chances.

Processing Time in 2025

Canada visitor visa processing times for Pakistanis in 2025 are typically 4–8 weeks. However, IRCC times fluctuate — always apply at least 10–12 weeks before your planned travel date. Biometrics appointment in Islamabad must be completed before processing begins.

Family Sponsorship Visitor Visa

If a Canadian PR or citizen is inviting you, their invitation letter and financial documents are a key part of your file. The letter should include:

  • Their relationship to you
  • Their Canadian address and contact details
  • Duration and purpose of your stay
  • Confirmation that they will cover your expenses (if applicable)
  • Copies of their Canadian PR card or passport

Schengen Visa Checklist for Pakistanis: Every Document Explained

Missing even one document from your Schengen visa application can cause an immediate refusal. This is the complete, document-by-document checklist for Pakistani applicants — with exact specifications for each item.

📋 Quick Note: Requirements may vary slightly by embassy (German, French, Italian, Spanish, etc.). Always check the specific consulate website for your destination country. Travxo verifies your complete file against the current requirements of your specific embassy before submission.

🪪 Personal Documents

  • Valid Passport — Original passport + all old passports. Must have minimum 2 blank pages and be valid at least 3 months beyond your return date from Schengen.
  • Passport-size Photos — 2 recent photos, 35×45mm, white background, taken within last 6 months. Must meet ICAO biometric standards.
  • CNIC — Photocopy of front and back of your Pakistani National Identity Card.
  • Visa Application Form — Completed, signed, and dated. Download from the specific embassy's website.

✈️ Travel Documents

  • Flight Itinerary — Confirmed or dummy round-trip flight reservation showing your exact entry and exit dates from Schengen. Must be in your name.
  • Hotel / Accommodation Proof — Hotel bookings, Airbnb confirmations, or a host's invitation letter with their ID. Must cover every night of your stay.
  • Travel Insurance — Minimum €30,000 medical coverage, valid for all 27 Schengen countries, covering your entire trip duration. Must show insurer name and policy number.
  • Day-by-Day Itinerary — A clear written travel plan for each day: which city, what activities, which hotel. Shows genuine tourist intent.

💰 Financial Documents

  • Bank Statement — Last 6 Months — Original, bank-stamped. Must show sufficient consistent balance (recommended minimum: €50–70 per day of stay). No unexplained large deposits.
  • Bank Certificate / Letter — On bank letterhead confirming your name, account number, account type, and current available balance.
  • Salary Slips — Last 3–6 months salary slips if employed. Shows regular monthly income.
  • FBR Tax Returns — Last 1–2 years if self-employed or business owner. Demonstrates established financial presence.
  • Sponsor's Financial Documents — If someone else is funding your trip: their bank statements, employment letter, and a signed affidavit of financial support.

💼 Employment / Business Documents

  • Employment Letter / NOC — On company letterhead: your designation, salary, confirmed leave dates, and a statement guaranteeing your return to employment. Must be signed by HR or management.
  • Business Ownership Proof — If self-employed: NTN certificate, SECP registration, chamber of commerce membership.
  • Leave Approval Letter — From your employer confirming leave is approved for exact travel dates.

🏠 Ties to Pakistan (Critical)

  • Property Documents — Ownership papers for house, plot, or commercial property in your name or immediate family.
  • Family Ties Evidence — Marriage certificate, children's birth certificates, or NADRA family registration certificate showing dependents in Pakistan.
  • Previous Travel History — Copies of all prior visas and entry/exit stamps from any country. Schengen, US, UK, Canadian visas significantly strengthen your application.
